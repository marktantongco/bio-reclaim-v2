## 2. Methamphetamine: The "Shabu" Crisis

### 2.1 What is Methamphetamine?

**Chemical Name:** Methamphetamine
**Common Names:** Shabu, Meth, Crystal, Ice, Glass, Speed, Crank
**Classification:** Schedule II substance (high abuse potential)

**Chemical Composition:**
- Similar to amphetamine with additional methyl group (CH3)
- Crosses blood-brain barrier more rapidly
- More intense dopamine release than amphetamine

**Forms:**
| Form | Names | Appearance | Purity | Methods of Use |
|------|-------|------------|--------|----------------|
| Powder | Speed, Go-ee, Whiz | White/off-white powder | 10-20% | Snorting, swallowing, injecting |
| Base | Paste, Wax, Pure | Damp/oily, white-brown | 20-30% | Injecting, swallowing |
| Crystal | Ice, Glass, Shabu | Translucent crystals | >80% | Smoking, injecting |
| Pills | - | Tablets | Varies | Swallowing |

**Methods of Use:**
- **Smoking/Injecting:** Immediate intense "rush" (highly addictive)
- **Snorting/Swallowing:** Less intense but longer-lasting high (up to 12 hours)

### 2.2 Social Impact in the Philippines

**Prevalence:**
- **93.65%** of total treatment admissions (2024 DDB data)
- **3.6%** of in-school adolescents used in past 30 days (2019)
- **40.08%** first tried between ages 15-19

**Demographics:**
- Higher among boys (19.1%) than girls (8.6%)
- Average family income: ₱13,287.61/month (~$229 USD)
- Concentrated in lower-income households

**Economic Impact:**
- Production cost: ₱2,000/kilo
- Street price: ₱3,000-5,000/gram
- Used as "pampagilas" (performance enhancer) in informal economy

**"Pampagilas" Myth:**
- **Perception:** Enhances stamina, strength, confidence for work
- **Reality:** Temporary boost followed by devastating long-term consequences
- **Consequences:** Addiction, health deterioration, social isolation, economic ruin

### 2.3 Understanding User Behaviors and Risks

**The High-Low Cycle:**

1. **Rush** (smoking/injecting): Intense pleasure, 20 minutes
2. **High**: Powerful, energetic, 18-24 hours
3. **Tweaking**: Paranoia, anxiety, hallucinations, insomnia
4. **Crash**: Deep sleep, 1-3 days

**Physical Side Effects:**
- Rapid heart rate, increased blood pressure
- Stroke risk (damaged blood vessels in brain)
- Cardiovascular collapse
- Hyperthermia (overheating)
- "Meth mouth" (severe dental problems)
- Extreme weight loss

**Psychological Effects:**
- Anxiety, confusion, insomnia
- Paranoia, aggression, hallucinations
- Psychotic features (can persist for months/years)
- Post-Acute Withdrawal Syndrome (PAWS)

**Long-Term Complications:**
- **50% of dopamine-producing cells can be destroyed**
- Increased Parkinson's disease risk
- Chronic anxiety, depression, psychosis
- Cognitive and motor impairments

### 2.4 New Frontiers in Recovery

**Emerging Pharmacological Treatments:**

1. **Naltrexone + Bupropion Combination**
   - Extended-release injectable naltrexone + oral bupropion
   - More effective than placebo (ADAPT-2 trial)
   - Reduces cravings and withdrawal dysphoria

2. **Lisdexamfetamine**
   - ADHD medication being studied for meth withdrawal
   - Reduces withdrawal symptoms and cravings
   - Could be world's first meth withdrawal treatment

3. **Atypical Antipsychotics**
   - Olanzapine, Quetiapine
   - Manages psychotic symptoms during withdrawal

**Psychosocial Therapies:**

- **Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT)** - Changes thought/behavior patterns
- **The Matrix Model** - 16-week comprehensive outpatient program
- **Contingency Management** - Rewards for positive behaviors
- **Motivational Interviewing** - Builds motivation for change
- **Mindfulness-Based Relapse Prevention (MBRP)** - Combines CBT with meditation

**Addressing Co-occurring Disorders:**
- Integrated treatment for depression, anxiety, PTSD, bipolar disorder
- Combination of medication + therapy
- Trauma-focused therapies (EMDR)

## 3. Ecstasy (MDMA): The Party Drug's Hidden Dangers

### 3.1 What is Ecstasy?

**Chemical Name:** 3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine (MDMA)
**Common Names:** Ecstasy, E, X, XTC, Molly, Adam
**Classification:** Schedule I substance (high abuse potential, no medical use)

**Chemical Composition:**
- Similar to methamphetamine + mescaline
- Increases serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine release
- **Higher affinity for serotonin transporter (SERT)**

**Effects:**
- Euphoria, emotional warmth
- Increased energy, empathy
- Distorted time/sensory perception
- Enhanced tactile sensitivity

**Common Misconception vs. Reality:**

| Misconception | Reality |
|---------------|---------|
| "Safe party drug" | Can cause serotonin syndrome, anxiety, aggression |
| "Non-addictive" | Causes psychological dependence |
| "Pure MDMA on street" | Often adulterated with meth, ketamine, caffeine |
| "Harmless with alcohol" | Increases dehydration, overheating risk |

### 3.2 Social Impact and User Behavior

**Prevalence in Philippines:**
- **1.3%** of in-school adolescents used in past 30 days (2019)
- **249,458 ecstasy pills** seized in 5-year period
- Popular in urban nightlife and music scene

**Social/Emotional Appeal:**
- "Hug drug" effect - euphoria, empathy, closeness
- Reduces inhibitions, increases tactile sensitivity
- Associated with raves, nightclubs, social gatherings

**Risks and Adverse Effects:**

**Immediate:**
- Overdose (can be fatal)
- Hyperthermia (organ failure risk)
- Seizures, loss of consciousness
- Irregular heartbeat
- Serotonin syndrome

**Long-Term:**
- Depletes serotonin stores
- Persistent depression, anxiety
- Memory problems, cognitive deficits
- Liver, kidney, heart damage
- Neurotoxicity to serotonergic neurons

### 3.3 Paths to Recovery

**Detoxification:**
- Not physically painful but psychologically distressing
- Symptoms: Anxiety, confusion, fatigue, insomnia, depression
- Duration: 3-4 days to clear system, weeks for psychological effects
- Medically monitored detox recommended

**Therapeutic Approaches:**
- **Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT)** - Changes thought patterns
- **Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)** - Emotional regulation
- **Motivational Interviewing (MI)** - Builds motivation
- **Family Therapy** - Improves communication
- **Group Therapy** - Peer support
- Alternative: Art therapy, music therapy, equine therapy

**Long-Term Recovery:**
- Strong support network (NA, SMART Recovery)
- Healthy routine (exercise, hobbies)
- Identify and avoid triggers
- Relapse is not failure - opportunity to learn

## 4. Marijuana (Cannabis): Beyond the Smoke

### 4.1 Understanding Marijuana

**Primary Component:** Delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol (THC)
**Other Cannabinoids:** CBD (non-psychoactive, therapeutic potential)
**Global Users:** 244 million

**Forms and Methods:**
- **Dried flowers/buds** - Smoked (joint, pipe, bong)
- **Hashish** - Concentrated resin (higher THC)
- **Hash oil** - Concentrated extract
- **Edibles** - Brownies, cookies, candies (delayed, potent effects)

**Medicinal vs. Recreational Debate:**

**Proponents:**
- Relief for chronic pain, epilepsy, MS, chemotherapy side effects
- Interacts with endocannabinoid system

**Opponents:**
- Insufficient scientific evidence
- Abuse potential
- Long-term health effects unclear
- Message to youth

### 4.2 Social Impact in Philippines

**Prevalence:**
- **24.96%** of drug-related admissions (2024)
- **7.9%** of high school students used in past 30 days (2019)
- **Second most commonly abused drug** after methamphetamine

**Co-use with Alcohol:**
- Substantial co-use among adolescents
- Amplifies negative effects
- Risk factors: Male, older age, poor sleep, lack of parental supervision

**Legal Framework:**
- Currently illegal for all purposes
- Growing movement for medical legalization
- Severe penalties for possession/use
- Debate over declassification and decriminalization

### 4.3 Cannabis Use Disorder (CUD)

**DSM-5 Diagnostic Criteria (2+ symptoms in 12 months):**
- Taking more than intended
- Persistent desire/unsuccessful efforts to cut down
- Great deal of time obtaining/using/recovering
- Craving
- Failure to fulfill obligations
- Continued use despite social/interpersonal problems
- Giving up activities
- Use in hazardous situations
- Continued use despite physical/psychological problems
- **Tolerance**
- **Withdrawal**

**Severity:** Mild (2-3), Moderate (4-5), Severe (6+)

**Common Misconceptions:**

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| "Not addictive" | Psychological dependence is real |
| "Soft drug, harmless" | Impairs memory, cognition, increases mental health risks |
| "No withdrawal" | Cravings, irritability, anxiety, sleep disturbances |

### 4.4 Treatment and Recovery

**Psychotherapeutic Interventions:**
- **CBT** - Changes negative patterns
- **Motivational Enhancement Therapy (MET)** - Resolves ambivalence
- **CBT + MET combination** - Most effective (9-session program)
- **Contingency Management** - Rewards for abstinence
- **Multidimensional Family Therapy (MDFT)** - For adolescents
- **Mindfulness-based interventions** - Reduces cravings

## 5. GHB (Liquid G): The Predatory Drug

### 5.1 What is GHB?

**Chemical Name:** Gamma-Hydroxybutyrate (GHB)
**Formula:** C4H8O3
**Common Names:** Liquid G, G, Georgia Home Boy, Grievous Bodily Harm, Easy Lay

**Properties:**
- Clear, odorless liquid
- Slightly salty taste
- CNS depressant
- Naturally occurs in brain (small amounts)

**Prescription Form:** Sodium oxybate (Xyrem) - FDA-approved for narcolepsy

**Common Uses:**
- **Recreational:** Euphoric, sedative effects
- **Criminal:** "Date rape" drug (easily spiked in drinks)
- **Self-medication:** Anxiety, depression, opioid withdrawal (NOT evidence-based)

### 5.2 Dangers and Withdrawal

**Acute Risks:**
- Disorientation, loss of coordination
- Memory loss
- Hallucinations, confusion, vomiting, seizures
- **Overdose:** Coma, respiratory depression, death
- Synergistic effects with alcohol/other depressants

**Withdrawal Syndrome (SEVERE):**
- Tremors, tachycardia, hypertension
- Hyperthermia, hallucinations, psychosis
- **Life-threatening:** Seizures, rhabdomyolysis, organ failure
- Similar to alcohol/benzodiazepine withdrawal
- Delirium tremens possible

**Counter-Intuitive Facts:**
- Naturally occurring in body BUT dangerous when taken exogenously
- Prescription medication (Xyrem) BUT Schedule I controlled substance
- "Party drug" BUT also "date rape" drug

### 5.3 Treatment Approaches

**Managing Severe Withdrawal:**
- Medical emergency requiring close supervision
- **First-line:** High-dose benzodiazepines (diazepam, lorazepam)
- **Alternatives:** Barbiturates (phenobarbital), Baclofen (GABA-B agonist)
- Combination medications may be necessary

**Pharmacological Interventions:**
- No FDA-approved medications for GHB addiction
- **Benzodiazepines** - Acute withdrawal management
- **Antidepressants (SSRIs)** - Co-occurring depression/anxiety
- **Gabapentin** - Reduces cravings, promotes abstinence

**Long-Term Recovery:**
- CBT, group therapy, family therapy
- Support groups (NA)
- Comprehensive, holistic approach

## 6. Cocaine: The Stimulant Trap

### 6.1 Understanding Cocaine

**Source:** Coca plant (South America)
**Classification:** Schedule II substance
**Mechanism:** Blocks dopamine reuptake → dopamine buildup → intense pleasure

**Forms:**
1. **Powder Cocaine** - White powder (snorted, injected)
   - High lasts 15-30 minutes
2. **Crack Cocaine** - Rock crystal (smoked)
   - Intense, immediate high (few minutes)

**Binge-and-Crash Cycle:**
1. **High:** Intense euphoria, energy (short-lived)
2. **Crash:** Depression, anxiety, fatigue
3. **Craving:** Strong desire for more
4. **Binge:** Compulsive use period

### 6.2 Social Impact and Rising Use

**Trends in Philippines:**
- Growing problem among affluent, urban areas
- Status symbol among elite
- Increased PDEA seizures in recent years

**Glamorization:**
- Movies, music, TV portray as success symbol
- False sense of security
- Normalization of use
- Challenges prevention efforts

### 6.3 Risks and Health Consequences

**Physical Effects:**
- Increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure
- Constricted blood vessels
- Heart attack, stroke, aortic rupture risk
- Respiratory problems (smoking)
- Asthma, respiratory failure

**Psychological Effects:**
- Anxiety, paranoia, panic attacks
- Exacerbates mental health conditions
- **Cocaine-induced psychosis:** Hallucinations, delusions

**Overdose Risk:**
- Can occur anytime, even in experienced users
- Signs: Chest pain, difficulty breathing, seizures, unconsciousness
- **Cocaethylene** (cocaine + alcohol): Toxic, increases sudden death risk

### 6.4 Treatment Directions

**Psychosocial Interventions:**
- **CBT** - Changes thought/behavior patterns
- **Contingency Management** - Rewards for abstinence

**Pharmacotherapy (Research Stage):**
- **Disulfiram** - Alcohol addiction medication
- **Modafinil** - Narcolepsy medication
- **Topiramate** - Anticonvulsant

**Combination Therapies:**
- Medication + CBT + CM
- Most promising approach
- Better outcomes than single approach

## 7. Holistic Recovery: Nourishing the Mind and Body

### 7.1 New Mental Health Approaches

**Mindfulness and Meditation:**
- **Mindfulness:** Present-moment awareness (non-judgmental)
- **MBRP (Mindfulness-Based Relapse Prevention):** CBT + meditation
- Benefits: Manages cravings, reduces stress, increases self-awareness

**CBT Evolution:**
- **Technology-assisted CBT:** Smartphone apps, online programs
- **Compassion-Focused Therapy (CFT):** Self-compassion, acceptance
- Reduces shame, self-criticism

**Self-Care and Community:**
- **Self-care:** Sleep, nutrition, exercise, hobbies
- **Community support:** Family, friends, therapist, support groups, faith community
- Provides belonging, accountability, encouragement

### 7.2 Food for the Brain

**Essential Nutrients:**
- **Omega-3 fatty acids** (EPA/DHA): Brain health, reduces inflammation
- **B vitamins:** Energy, neurotransmitter synthesis
- **Antioxidants:** Protects brain from free radicals
- **Protein:** Neurotransmitter production, tissue repair

**Brain-Boosting Foods:**
- **Fatty fish:** Salmon, mackerel, sardines (omega-3s)
- **Blueberries:** Antioxidants
- **Turmeric:** Curcumin (anti-inflammatory)
- **Broccoli:** Antioxidants, vitamin K
- **Pumpkin seeds:** Zinc (nerve signaling)

**Foods to AVOID:**
- Processed foods (sugar, unhealthy fats, sodium)
- Sugary drinks (blood sugar spikes)
- Excessive caffeine (trigger, sleep interference)
- **Alcohol** (depressant, relapse trigger)

### 7.3 Food for Hormones

**Hormonal Impact of Substance Use:**
- **Alcohol:** Interferes with testosterone, increases estrogen (men)
- **Opioids:** Suppresses sex hormones, infertility
- **Stimulants:** Disrupts adrenal glands, chronic stress/fatigue

**Dietary Strategies for Balance:**
- Balanced diet (all food groups)
- Limit sugar and refined carbs
- Adequate fiber (regulates blood sugar, removes excess hormones)
- Healthy fats (avocados, nuts, seeds)
- Stress management (exercise, meditation, yoga)

### 7.4 Supplements in Recovery

**Beneficial Supplements:**
- **Multivitamin/mineral:** Fills nutritional gaps
- **Omega-3 fatty acids:** If not eating fatty fish
- **B-complex vitamins:** Energy, neurotransmitter support
- **Magnesium:** Reduces anxiety, improves sleep
- **Probiotics:** Restores gut microbiome

**Supplements to AVOID:**
- **Kava:** Liver toxicity, drug interactions
- **Kratom:** Opioid-like, addictive
- **St. John's Wort:** Medication interactions
- **High-dose vitamins/minerals:** Can be harmful
